Poem

So I began my journey here
When I was in third grade
I learned to ride a unicycle
As well as how to braid

The next two years they were for me
Comparatively bland
The most exciting thing I did
Was break a music stand

In sixth grade I remember
A story Ms Pothof read
And something inappropriate
Dakota might have said

And as for seventh grade
It wasn't that much fun
Apart from playing basketball with
Ben's mom's dad's grandson

And then as an eighth grader
The caterpillars arrived
To be completely honest
I'm not sure how I survived

I played a lot of ping-pong
When I was in ninth grade
Won twelve hundred dollars off Zhumabek
Which he still hasn't paid

My 10th grade year I ended
Abroad in Germany
My experience there was dampered by
Die Korona Pandemie

11th grade was somber
Until we got off Zoom
I was grateful to be back in person
So were others I assume

This final year's been quite severe
I've had to ponder my career
Whether to be a black marketeer or
become a civil engineer
